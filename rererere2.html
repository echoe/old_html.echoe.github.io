I stopped at the apex and realized that everybody else in the store was stopped, was letting the spore do what it wanted. It seemed inevitable. But I wouldn't let it happen.\n I abandoned my cart inside – no stealing – and started to run out of the store, through the parking lot instead of to my car, blue spores falling all around me as though they were a heavy rain. “Fuck.”\n I held my head as I moved into a sitting position. Everything hurt before, and it just continued to hurt. The theme of the week, I guessed. Not like I wasn't used to things hurting, but usually it was more figurative than this, and I really just wanted to continue the earlier process I'd been following of throwing up my organs into a bucket orally. I felt like I was outside of my own body, somehow, floating on a current of air around myself and yet connected, in that I was able to feel every sensation that I felt. And I felt sick. Violently, woefully sick, like how I felt when I had the 'food poisoning', like how I felt when I was there, earlier, like everything was wrong … but there was something else there, from the horror and the pain, something else that was lurking behind it that I didn't quite recognize but later pegged as a note of acceptance, of relief.
